Introduction

Learning Quranic Arabic is the key to understanding the powerful messages in the Quranic verses and pondering their depth.

This course focuses on learning Arabic grammar rules and understanding the meaning of numerous key Quranic words. This allows the student to gain an insight into the verses. We aim to make the learning journey easy and enjoyable till proficiency.

Prerequisite: To start this course, students should already be able to read the Quran fluently.

Lesson 1: The Arabic Word (أَقْسَامُ الكَلِمَة)

The Word (الْكَلِمَة)

A word is the fundamental unit of spoken or written language. In Arabic, any word can be one of three types:

كِتَابٌ عَلِمَ مِنْ

A book (Noun) He knew (Verb) From (Particle)

1. The Noun (الاسْم)

A noun is a word that names a person, animal, place, object, or things. The Arabic noun is a broad category that includes nouns, pronouns, adjectives, and verbal nouns.

Examples:

Arabic English
رَبٌّ Lord
إِنْسَانٌ Man
قَلَمٌ Pen
عَبْدٌ Servant
هُدَى Guidance
تَقْوَى Righteousness
اللهُ Allah
اسْمٌ Name
أَنَا I
هَذَا This
أَيْنَ Where
الَّذِي Who

2. The Verb (الْفِعْل)

A verb is a word that indicates an action confined to a specific time (past, present, or command).

Examples:

Arabic English
خَلَقَ He created (Past)
يَنْهَى He forbids (Present)
اقْرَأْ Read! (Command)

3. The Particle (الْحَرْف)

A particle is a word which does not convey a complete meaning when it stands alone. It gives meaning to a sentence only when it is used with a verb or a noun. The particle does not indicate the past, present or command tense. It includes categories like prepositions, conjunctions, connectors, particles of negation, and so on.

Examples:

Arabic English
بِـ With / in
مِنْ From
إِنَّ Indeed
عَلَى Upon
عَنْ About

Lesson 2: The Signs of a Noun (عَلَامَاتُ الاِسْمِ)

How to Recognize a Noun

For each type of word, there are certain signs that tell you its type immediately. A noun in Arabic is recognized by one of the following signs.

1. The Taa Marbutah (ـة)

Any word that ends with the Taa Marbutah is a noun.

لَيْلَةٌ A night

2. The Tanween (ـً ـٍ ـٌ)

Any word that ends with Tanween (double vowel marking) is a noun.

كِتَابٌ A book

3. The "Al" Prefix (الـ)

Any word that begins with the definite article 'Al' is a noun.

الْقَمَرُ The moon

4. Preceded by a Preposition

Any word that comes after a preposition (حرف الجر) is a noun.

فِي الْبَيْتِ In the house

5. Ends with Kasrah (ـِ)

If a word ends with a Kasrah, it is a noun (often due to a preceding preposition or Idafa).

بِسْمِ اللهِ In the name of Allah

6. The Idafa (الإضافة)

A word that is possessed in a possessive phrase (Mudaf) is always a noun.

كِتَابُ الطَّالِبِ The student's book

7. The Nida' (النداء)

A word that follows a particle of calling (like Yaa) is a noun.

يَا رَجُلُ O man!

Examples from Surah At-Tin

Notice the colored words in the Ayahs and how they match the signs of a noun explained below.

وَالتِّينِ وَالزَّيْتُونِ

By the fig and the olive.

  • التِّينِ is a noun because it starts with 'Al' and ends with a Kasrah.
  • الزَّيْتُونِ is a noun because it starts with 'Al' and ends with a Kasrah.

لَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا الْإِنسَانَ فِي أَحْسَنِ تَقْوِيمٍ

We have certainly created man in the best stature;

  • الْإِنسَانَ is a noun because it starts with 'Al'.
  • أَحْسَنِ is a noun because it is preceded by the preposition 'فِي' and ends with a Kasrah.
  • تَقْوِيمٍ is a noun because it ends with Tanween.

Lesson 3: Definite & Indefinite Nouns (النَّكِرَةُ وَالْمَعْرِفَةُ)

The Four Characteristics of a Noun

Every noun in Arabic must have four characteristics. In this lesson, we focus on the first one: being either Definite (مَعْرِفَة) or Indefinite (نَكِرَة).
  1. Definiteness: Definite or Indefinite.
  2. Case Ending: Nominative, Accusative, or Genitive.
  3. Gender: Masculine or Feminine.
  4. Number: Singular, Dual, or Plural.

1. The Indefinite Noun (النَّكِرَةُ)

An indefinite noun refers to a non-specific person, animal, or object. Think of it as using "a" or "an" in English.

Key Signs:

  • It does not start with "Al" (ال).
  • It usually ends with Tanween (e.g., كِتَابٌ, بَيْتًا, نَارٍ).

Examples:

Arabic English
كِتَابٌ Book
بَابٌ Door
جُوعٌ Hunger
طَيْرًا Birds
حِجَارَةٍ Stones
نَارٌ Fire
أَفْئِدَةٌ Hearts
إِنْسَانٌ Man
ذَرَّةٍ Atom
رَسُولٌ Apostle
جَنَّاتٌ Gardens

2. The Definite Noun (الْمَعْرِفَة)

A definite noun refers to a specific person, animal, or object. Think of it as using "the" in English, or referring to something specific by name.

Type 1: Using the Definite Article (ال)

The most common way to make a noun definite is by adding "Al" (ال) to the beginning. When you add "Al", you must remove the Tanween from the end.

Indefinite Definite Meaning
صِرَاطٌ الصِّرَاطُ A path → The path
فَتْحٌ الْفَتْحُ A victory → The victory
يَتِيمٌ الْيَتِيمُ An orphan → The orphan
بَيْتٌ الْبَيْتُ A house → The house
خَوْفٌ الْخَوْفُ A fear → The fear

Type 2: Proper Names (أَسْمَاءُ العَلَم)

The names of specific people or places are automatically definite without needing "Al".

Arabic English
أَحْمَدُ Ahmed
فَاطِمَةُ Fatema
مِصْرُ Egypt
بَيْرُوتُ Beirut

Type 3: Possessive Phrases (الْإِضَافَة)

A noun becomes definite when it is in a possessive phrase (Idafa), such as "the book of Allah" or "your book".

Arabic English
عَبْدُ اللَّهِ Slave of Allah
كِتَابُ اللَّهِ Book of Allah
سَبِيلِي My way
يَمِينِي My right
رَبُّكَ Your Lord
نَارُ اللَّهِ Fire of Allah
أَصْحَابُ الْقُبُورِ People of the grave
كِتَابُكُمْ Your book

Quranic Examples: Definite & Indefinite Nouns

قُلْ يَا أَيُّهَا الْكَافِرُونَ

Say, "O disbelievers". (109:1)

  • الْكَافِرُونَ is a Definite Noun because it starts with "Al".

إِنَّا أَعْطَيْنَاكَ الْكَوْثَرَ

Indeed, We have granted you, [O Muhammad], al-Kawthar. (108:1)

  • الْكَوْثَرَ is a Definite Noun because it starts with "Al".

إِيلَافِهِمْ رِحْلَةَ الشِّتَاءِ وَالصَّيْفِ

Their accustomed security [in] the caravan of winter and summer. (106:2)

  • رِحْلَةَ الشِّتَاءِ is a Definite Noun because it is a possessive phrase (Idafa).
  • الصَّيْفِ is a Definite Noun because it starts with "Al".

وَأَرْسَلَ عَلَيْهِمْ طَيْرًا أَبَابِيلَ

And He sent against them birds in flocks. (105:3)

  • طَيْرًا and أَبَابِيلَ are Indefinite Nouns because they end with Tanween.

وَيَمْنَعُونَ الْمَاعُونَ

And withhold [simple] assistance. (107:7)

  • الْمَاعُونَ is a Definite Noun because it starts with "Al".

تَرْمِيهِم بِحِجَارَةٍ مِّن سِجِّيلٍ

Striking them with stones of hard clay. (105:4)

  • بِحِجَارَةٍ and سِجِّيلٍ are Indefinite Nouns because they end with Tanween.

وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا الْحُطَمَةُ

And what can make you know what is the Crusher? (104:5)

  • الْحُطَمَةُ is a Definite Noun because it starts with "Al".

فَهُوَ فِي عِيشَةٍ رَّاضِيَةٍ

He will be in a pleasant life. (101:7)

  • عِيشَةٍ and رَّاضِيَةٍ are Indefinite Nouns because they end with Tanween.

فَذَٰلِكَ الَّذِي يَدُعُّ الْيَتِيمَ

For that is the one who drives away the orphan. (107:2)

  • الْيَتِيمَ is a Definite Noun because it starts with "Al".

الَّذِي جَمَعَ مَالًا وَعَدَّدَهُ

Who collects wealth and [continuously] counts it. (104:2)

  • مَالًا is an Indefinite Noun because it ends with Tanween.

وَلَا يَحُضُّ عَلَىٰ طَعَامِ الْمِسْكِينِ

And does not encourage the feeding of the poor. (107:3)

  • طَعَامِ الْمِسْكِينِ is a Definite Noun because it is a possessive phrase (Idafa).

رَسُولٌ مِّنَ اللَّهِ يَتْلُو صُحُفًا مُّطَهَّرَةً

A Messenger from Allah, reciting purified scriptures. (98:2)

  • رَسُولٌ, صُحُفًا, and مُّطَهَّرَةً are Indefinite Nouns (ending with Tanween).
  • اللَّهِ is a Definite Noun (Proper Name).

الَّتِي تَطَّلِعُ عَلَى الْأَفْئِدَةِ

Which mounts directed at the hearts. (104:7)

  • الْأَفْئِدَةِ is a Definite Noun because it starts with "Al".

فَمَن يَعْمَلْ مِثْقَالَ ذَرَّةٍ خَيْرًا يَرَهُ

So whoever does an atom's weight of good will see it. (99:7)

  • ذَرَّةٍ and خَيْرًا are Indefinite Nouns because they end with Tanween.

حَتَّىٰ زُرْتُمُ الْمَقَابِرَ

Until you visit the graveyards. (102:2)

  • الْمَقَابِرَ is a Definite Noun because it starts with "Al".

كَلَّا لَوْ تَعْلَمُونَ عِلْمَ الْيَقِينِ

No! If you only knew with knowledge of certainty... (99:5)

  • عِلْمَ الْيَقِينِ is a Definite Noun because it is a possessive phrase (Idafa).

Exercise 2: Definite or Indefinite?

In the following Ayahs, define whether the bold words are definite or indefinite.

مِنَ الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ

سَيَصْلَىٰ نَارًا ذَاتَ لَهَبٍ

فِي جِيدِهَا حَبْلٌ مِّن مَّسَدٍ

إِذَا جَاءَ نَصْرُ اللَّهِ وَالْفَتْحُ

وَرَأَيْتَ النَّاسَ يَدْخُلُونَ فِي دِينِ اللَّهِ أَفْوَاجًا

الَّذِي أَطْعَمَهُم مِّن جُوعٍ وَآمَنَهُم مِّن خَوْفٍ

أَلَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ فَعَلَ رَبُّكَ بِأَصْحَابِ الْفِيلِ

  • الْجِنَّةِ / النَّاسِ / الْفَتْحُ / الْفِيلِ: Definite (starts with "Al").
  • نَارًا / لَهَبٍ / حَبْلٌ / مَّسَدٍ / أَفْوَاجًا / جُوعٍ / خَوْفٍ: Indefinite (ends with Tanween).
  • نَصْرُ اللَّهِ / دِينِ اللَّهِ / بِأَصْحَابِ الْفِيلِ: Definite (possessive phrase - Idafa).

Exercise 5: Complete the Sentences

Choose the correct definite word from the brackets to complete the Ayah.

قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ _______ (النَّاسِ - نَاسٍ)

فَسَبِّحْ بِحَمْدِ _______ وَاسْتَغْفِرْهُ (رَبِّكَ - رَبٍّ)

لَكُمْ _______ وَلِيَ دِينِ (دِينُكُمْ - دِينٌ)

إِنَّ _______ لَفِي خُسْرٍ (الْإِنسَانَ - إِنسَانٍ)
